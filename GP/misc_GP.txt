When Pareto wrote, “History is the graveyard of aristocracies,” he meant that no ruling class remains dominant forever. Throughout history, political, economic, and social elites rise to power, consolidate authority, and eventually decline as they become rigid, complacent, or disconnected from society. They are then replaced by new elites who take their place. For Pareto, history is less a story of rule by the masses and more a continuous cycle of elite circulation.
The powerful may change, but power itself tends to remain concentrated in the hands of a minority.
